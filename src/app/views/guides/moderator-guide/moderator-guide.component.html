Moderator Guide

As a moderator, your job is to ban/unban people, reset player passwords when needed and create accounts for players when registrations are closed. This means that your role is to support people in need. You also get a special icon on your ID, like this:

Also make sure to review the Verifier Guide, as it also applies to you, except that moderators can change most verifications.

And don't forget that all of your actions are logged and viewable in the logs, as well as the actions of other users.

How to Ban / Unban

Next to the star and block buttons next to a players ID is the button to ban a player. You can only ban normal players or verifiers, not other moderators or admins.

How to Reset Passwords

The same way normal players reset their passwords (see Profile Guide), except you can reset passwords on other player profiles as well.

Account Creation

In case the site is under attack (e.g. account creation bot, etc.) and registrations are closed, you can still create accounts for players by using the Advanced Account Creator in the Control Panel.

To create an account for a player, follow these steps:

1 Go to the Advanced Account Creator

Click the in the navigation bar on top to go to the Control Panel which contains the Advanced Account Creator.

2 Create an Account

Fill in the form to create an account and use a "one-time-use" password for the player to log into the account. Once the player logged in they can reset their own password on their own profile. If they forget the password or can't log in, you can always reset their password.

Only create accounts for actual players, e.g. people you meet in-game. Never create accounts based on posts in threads.

Special Permissions

As a moderator you also have permission to modify some player profile or session properties, such as player name or island name visibility.

You also have access to all Dodo Codes of sessions. Use this privilege carefully and only for moderation, e.g. checking if a Dodo Code is working.

Logs

As a moderator you also have permission to view the logs in the Control Panel.

You can filter by log level or search for specific logs, e.g. player or session IDs. Use these logs wisely.
